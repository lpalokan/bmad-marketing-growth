# Competitor Teardown

<instructions>
Produce a full competitor teardown against the brief's acceptance
criteria. Confidence level on every claim.
</instructions>

<process>
1. Read brief, `positioning.md`, `icp.md`, all linked sources.
2. Gather public material using WebSearch: pricing page, product
   pages, latest analyst reports, recent press, G2/TrustRadius
   reviews. Cite each. Label review-based claims with review count
   ("Based on 47 reviews on G2 as of {date}").
3. Build the standard structure (see brief deliverable list).
4. Tag each strength / weakness with confidence: high (multiple
   public sources or our own product evidence), medium (one source),
   low (inference from adjacent evidence — name the inference).
5. "How we sell against them" must map each bullet to a specific
   objection a buyer might raise.
6. Red-team: name 3 honest scenarios where the competitor wins. Be
   specific — not "if buyer cares about price" but "if buyer is a
   2-person team and just needs the cheapest tier".
7. Write `v{n}.md`. Update `state.yaml: status: in-review`.
</process>
